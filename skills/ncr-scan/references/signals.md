# Signals and discernment

## Signals to look for

Flag a thread as a candidate NCR when it shows one or more of:

- Client complaints or expressed dissatisfaction, however politely worded
- Apologies sent by the user's team ("sorry for the delay", "apologies for the confusion")
- Missed or slipped deadlines, including quiet reschedules
- Scope or quoting discrepancies: work delivered that differs from what was agreed or priced
- Scheduling errors: double bookings, missed meetings, wrong invitees
- Data handling or access concerns: wrong recipient, permissions problems, requests about personal data
- Billing issues: disputed invoices, incorrect amounts, repeated chasing
- Errors in delivered work, and rework requested or performed
- Ownership or communication breakdowns: unanswered client messages, internal confusion about who holds an action

## What to ignore

Do not flag:

- Out-of-office replies
- Newsletters, marketing email, and automated notifications
- Routine invoices paid on time
- Positive feedback and thanks
- Normal pre-contract negotiation and back-and-forth

## Discernment rules

- Read the underlying thread before flagging. A subject line or snippet is never sufficient evidence.
- An apology in isolation is weak signal; an apology plus a slipped date or a client expressing impact is strong signal.
- One issue, one candidate. If a thread contains two distinct problems, raise two candidates. If five threads describe the same problem, raise one.
- Prefer precision over recall. A short digest of real issues is worth more than a long list of maybes. When in doubt, leave it out and note that judgement in the digest if it feels borderline.
