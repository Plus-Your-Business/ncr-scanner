# Standard classification

Only apply the standards toggled on in the configuration. A candidate that fits none of the toggled standards but is still a real issue should be classified "General" with a plain issue category, not forced into a standard.

## ISO 9001 (Quality management)

Classify under 9001 when the issue concerns:

- Delivery quality: errors in delivered work, rework
- Deadlines: missed or slipped dates
- Scope: delivered work differing from what was agreed or quoted
- Communication: unanswered client messages, unclear ownership of actions
- Process: an agreed internal process not followed

## ISO 27001 (Information security management)

Classify under 27001 when the issue concerns:

- Data handling: information sent to the wrong recipient, mishandled files
- PII access: personal data seen by, or available to, people who should not have it
- DPAs and NDAs: missing, expired, or breached agreements
- Security incidents: suspected compromise, phishing affecting client work
- Access issues: wrong permissions, accounts not revoked, shared credentials

## ISO 42001 (AI management)

Classify under 42001 when the issue concerns:

- AI tool selection: tools adopted without assessment
- Onboarding: AI tools rolled out without agreed training or controls
- Governance: AI use outside agreed policy, unclear accountability for AI output
- Suitability: AI applied to tasks it was not assessed as fit for, or client concerns about AI use

## Multiple standards

If a candidate genuinely fits more than one toggled standard, list the primary standard first and mention the secondary in the issue summary. Do not duplicate the candidate.
